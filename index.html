What brought you here

The problems don't care
how long you've been at it.

New grad or seasoned clinician — the walls look different but they feel the same.

01 — Time

You're always running.

And never catching up.

VeteranBack-to-back patients, endless admin, no margin. Your schedule runs you — not the other way around.
New GradYou said yes to everything. Now your calendar owns you before you've even built anything.

02 — Strength

Your body is keeping score.

Neck, back, wrists. It adds up.

VeteranYou feel it by Thursday. The posture, the repetition, the years — your body has been quietly paying for this career.
New GradYou feel fine now. But the habits you build this decade determine what your body feels like at fifty.

03 — Money

The numbers don't add up.

After overhead. After loans. After taxes.

VeteranYou produce a lot. But what actually stays? The gap between gross and net is where dentist dreams go to die.
New GradSix figures of debt, entry-level pay, no roadmap. Nobody prepared you for the financial reality of this career.

04 — Freedom

This isn't what you pictured.

Not ungrateful. Just honest.

VeteranYou built a good life on paper. But 20 more years of this exact thing? That's not a plan — that's a sentence.
New GradYou chose dentistry for independence. Two years in, it already doesn't feel that way. You can course-correct now.
